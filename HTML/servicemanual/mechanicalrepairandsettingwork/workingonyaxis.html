Working on Y axis

Replacing the Y axis motor and gearbox

  • The machines main switch is switched off and secured against being switched on again . Means, Tools, Materials

  • Standard tool set, mat. no. 0135600.

  • Adjustable hook wrench 35-60 mm, mat. no. 2014343. Or hook wrench, mat. no.1655965.

  • Torque wrench, 20-120 Nm.

  • Torque wrench, 2.5-22.5 Nm.

  • If possible, disconnect coupling between engine and gearbox to remove both components separately.

    1. Remove the front covers of the cutting unit. Removing the Y axis motor and gear box .Front covers of cutting unit Fig. 449

      | |

  1. Remove the bellows.

  2. Disconnect the power cable and encoder cable from the motor. .Cables on Y motor Fig. 450

    |

  1. Remove the gearbox screw plug. .Fig. 451

    | |

|1 Motor screws (4x) |2 Screw plug

  1. Remove the grease distributor with the bracket. .Grease distributor Fig. 452

    |

  1. Push the Y slide so that the clamping screws of the coupling is accessible. .Clamping screw Fig. 453

    |

  1. Loosen the coupling clamping screws.

  2. Remove 4x motor screws (see Fig. 4-50, pg.xxx).

  3. Remove the motor.

  4. Loosen two long screws on the adjustment mechanism. .Fig. 454

    |

|1 Long screws (2x), adjustment mechanism |2 Gearbox screws (4x) |3 Flange mount screws (4x), already removed

  1. Remove 4x flange mount screws with washers.

  2. Remove the gearbox with the flange mount.

  3. Remove the gearbox from the flange mount.

  4. Clean the support areas on the Y slide, gearbox and motor. Installing Y axis gearbox and motor

  5. Find the marking for the highest point on Y rack. .Fig. 455

    |

  1. Starting from this marking, measure 110 mm in the direction of Y- and make another marking there on the Y rack using a permanent marker.

  2. Push the Y slide until its right edge is flush with the new marking. .Fig. 456

    |

  1. Install the new gearbox to the flange mount with Loctite 243.

    • Torque requirement: 17 Nm. .Fig. 457

      |

  1. Turn the pinion so that the marking faces upward and the hole for clamping screw on the right. .Fig. 458

    |

  1. Insert the gearbox into the Y slide without turning the pinion.

    • The housing opening must point to the right (Y=0). .Fig. 459

      |

  1. Insert 4x flange mount screws using Loctite 243, but do not yet tighten.

  2. Press the gearbox against the rack and tighten the screws to30 Nm. .Fig. 460

    |

  1. Push the Y slide so that the clamping screw of the coupling is accessible.

  2. Remove the flat key on the shaft of new motor with the help of M3 screw.

  3. Insert motor:

    • Align the keyway to the groove of coupling.

    • Motor cable interface must point to left (Y+). .Fig. 461

      |

  1. Insert 4x motor screws with Loctite 243 and tighten to 36 Nm.

  2. Tighten the coupling clamping screw to 35 Nm.

  3. Screw in and tighten the screw plug.

  4. Place the two wedge block. .Fig. 462

    |

  1. Tighten the two long screws above the wedge block without two much force.

  2. Fit the grease distributor with bracket.

  3. Plug the power cable and encoder cable into the motor and tighten with the hook wrench. .Fig. 463

    |

  1. Further work:

    • Reset error message E731.0 of the Inovance drive. (see Reset error message E731.0 of Inovance drive, pg. xxx)

    • Check and set the Y axis backlash. (see Checking and setting the Y axis backlash, pg. xxx)

    • Check and set the Y axis reference point offset value andfixed stops of the Y axis. (see Checking and setting the Y axis reference point off-set value, pg. xxx)

    • Set the Y axis limit switch. (see Checking and setting the Y axis limit switch, pg. xxx)

Checking and setting the Y axis backlash

Condition * Machine is switched on and ready for operation. Means, Tools, Material * Standard tool set, mat. no. 0135600. * Dial gauge 1/1000 with magnetic support, mat. no. 0002419. * Bluetooth dial gauge, mat. no. 2369502. * Permanent marker. * Ruler or measuring tape.

  1. Release the bellows on the left and right of the Y slide and push them to the side.

  2. Find the marking for the highest point on Y rack. .Fig. 464

    |

  1. Starting from this marking, measure 110 mm in the direction of Y- and make another marking there on the Y rack using a permanent marker.

  2. Push the Y slide until its right edge is flush with the new marking. .Fig. 465

    |

    • Check the backlash either with the help of current limit key switch or using the Bluetooth dial gauge.

  3. Remove the side enclosure. Check the backlash

  4. Remove the Y axis bellow mounting bracket. .Fig. 466

    |

  1. Remove the Y axis bellow supporting bar. .Fig. 467

    |

| a|

Risk of injury due to active drive when door is open. * Position the two mobile fixed stops before and after the X slide. * Position the two mobile fixed stops to the left and right of the Y slide. * Set the MOTOR CURRENT LIMIT key switch to right (ON).

  1. Position 2 mobile fixed stops before and behind the X slide.

    • L99s X/Y mobile fixed stop is different from PLLs. .Fig. 468

      | |

|Front |Rear

  1. Position 2 mobile fixed stops to the left and right of Y slide. .Fig. 469

    |

  1. Set the key switch for the motor current limit to right (ON). .Fig. 470

    |

  1. Switch on drives and open the safety door.

  2. Acknowledge the service mode is active at the bottom-left of HMI. .Fig. 471

    |

  1. Acknowledge the feedrate changed to 0 by PLC controller.

  2. Position the dial gauge against Y slide and zero. .Fig. 472

    |

  1. Push the Y slide in the + and directions with a force of about 3 kg and read off the backlash from the dial gauge. Set value: 0.010.005 mm.

  2. Position the Bluetooth dial gauge to the right of the Y slide and zero. Check the backlash with the Bluetooth dial gauge .Fig. 473

    |

  1. Close the safety door and drive on.

  2. In HMI, select Setup > settings.

  3. Enter 0.001 under all the Step column. .Fig. 474

    |

    • The Step value must be reset at the end.

  4. In jog mode, press -Y arrow button to move in the minus direction until the dial gauge deflects.
