Working on Z axis

Replacing the motor and spindle of the Z axis drive

  • Standard tool set, mat. no. 0135600.

  • Circlip plier.

  • Hook wrench 22-26 mm.

    1. Move the pallet out of the machine. Remove the motor

    2. Switch the machine’s main switch off and secure it against being switched on again.

    3. Remove the front cover of the cutting unit. .Front covers of the cutting unit Fig. 4‑7

      | |

  1. Unplug the cable and hoses on the Z slide:

    • Collision protection.

    • Compressed air.

    • Lubrication line.

    • Positioning laser diode.

    • Make a mark for collision protection cable to distinguish between collision protection cable and positioning diode cable when reassembling. .Fig. 4‑8

      | | |

|1 Compressed air |3 PE |5 Positioning diode |2 Collision protection |4 Lubrication line |

  1. Undo the chain (4x screws) at the front and rear side. .Fig. 4‑9

    |

  1. Tilt the chain to the side and place the cutting unit onto the motion unit.

    • Pay attention to the minimum bend radius of the LLK > 100 mm. .Cutting unit removed and set aside Fig. 4‑10

      |

  1. Remove the chain holding plate. .Fig. 4‑11

    |

  1. Unplug the Z motor cable. .Fig. 4‑12

    |

  1. Unscrew (17 Nm) motor screws (4x). .Fig. 4‑13

    |

| a|

Z slide may fall down due to screws of the coupling being loosen. Risk of defective machine. * Hold on the Z slide and lower it to the bottom carefully when undoing the coupling.

  1. Undo (14 Nm) the coupling on the spindle.

    • Rotate the motor to loosen the screws on the back of the coupling. .The coupling Fig. 4‑14

      |

  1. Lower the Z slide carefully to the lowest position.

  2. Pull out the motor.

  3. Remove the coupling on the spindle. Additional work for removing the spindle

  4. Remove two brackets on the cutting unit receptacle. .Fig. 4‑15

    |

  1. Undo (4.5 Nm) the screws (4x) on the ball nut. .Fig. 4‑16

    |

  1. Loosen the screws of guide carriages. .Fig. 4‑17

    | |

|1 Shoulder screws (2x) |2 Screws (14x)

  1. Remove the Z slide.

  2. Fix the ball nut on the spindle and the Z carriages.

    • Be careful of the ball nut and Z carriages falling. .Fig. 4‑18

      |

  1. Undo the jackscrew on the lock nut. .Fig. 4‑19

    |

  1. With the open-end wrench (size 14 mm) holding the groove of the spindle, and with the hook wrench, remove the lock nut. .Lock nut removal Fig. 4‑20

    |

  1. Dismantle the spray shield. .Fig. 4‑21

    |

  1. Remove the ball screw from the upper bearing mount by knocking lightly from above. .Fig. 4‑22

    |

  1. Remove the circlip under the lower bearing. .Fig. 4‑23

    |

  1. Remove the lower bearing.

  2. Tilt the spindle towards you and remove the spindle upwards. .Fig. 4‑24

    |

  1. Remove the cover on the upper bearing mount.

    • The screws on the bearing mount which are used for alignment must not be loosened, otherwise the spindle must be realigned in the X-Y plane.

    • The countersunk screws on the cover must not be loose-ned with the ball head of Allen key. .Fig. 4‑25

      | |

|1 Cover |2 Sleeve |3 Axial bearing (2x) |4 Bearing mount

  1. Replace the upper axial bearing (2x) and lower bearing (1x). Install the new spindle

    • Pay attention to the direction of two upper bearings. The mark on them must form an arrow downwards. .Fig. 4‑26

      |

  1. Remove the grease nipple from the old ball screw and install it on the new one, facing forward. .Fig. 4‑27

    |

  1. Clean all the installation contact surface.

  2. Insert the spindle through the bearing mount.

  3. Guide two upper bearings to the spindle.

  4. Guide a sleeve to the spindle.

  5. Mount the cover for the bearings.

    • Attach Loctite to the countersunk screws. .Fig. 4‑28

      | |

|1 Cover |2 Sleeve |3 Axial bearing (2x) |4 Bearing mount

  1. With the open-end wrench (size 14 mm) holding the groove of the spindle, and with the hook wrench, tighten the lock nut. .Fig. 4‑29

    |

  1. Tighten the jackscrew on the lock nut.

  2. Slightly knock the lower bearing from below to the spindle until the groove of spindle is visible.

  3. Fit circlip to the groove of spindle. .Fig. 4‑30

    |

  1. Mount the spray shield.

  2. Remove the fixture for ball nut and Z carriages.

  3. Install the Z slide:

    • Screw in 2x shoulder screws first, but not yet tighten.

    • Screw in 14x screws for carriages, but not yet tighten.

    • Screw in 4x screws for ball nut, but not yet tighten.

    • Move the Z slide up and down multiple times to be relieved of tension.

    • Tighten 2x shoulder screws by hand first, without Loctite.

    • Tighten 14x screws for carriages to 10 Nm, with Loctite.

    • Tighten 4x screws for ball nut to 4.5 Nm, with Loctite.

    • Do not use Loctite when assembling the shoulder screw. .Fig. 4‑31

      | |

|1 M5 screws (14x), carriages |2 M4 screws (4x), ball nut |3 Shoulder screws (2x) |

  1. Check if the Z slide move smoothly.

  2. Place the coupling.

  3. Using M3 screw, remove the flat key on the shaft of new motor. Install the new motor .Fig. 4‑32

    |

  1. Clean all the installation contact surface.

  2. Insert the motor into the YZ unit, aligning the slot of coupling to the key way.

    • The motor interface faces Y+ direction. .Fig. 4‑33

      |

  1. Tighten 2x screws on the coupling to 14 Nm.

  2. Rotate the motor to tighten the remaining 2 screws on the coupling to 14 Nm. .Fig. 4‑34

    |

  1. Rotate the motor back to original position.

  2. Tighten the screws of motor to 17 Nm.

  3. Perform the removal steps in the reverse order.

  4. Further work:
