Replacing cylinders and flaps of the suction channel

  • The machine is switched off and secured against restarting.

  • Both pallets are in the pallet changer.

  • The motion unit is at the max. X position. Means, Tools, Materials

  • Circlip plier.

  • Standard tool set, mat.no. 0135600. | a|

Risk of squeezing and crushing! Hand injuries. * Observe safety information of the manufacturer of the suction channel and suction system. * Wear personal protective equipment.

  1. Close the customers compressed air supply. Preparation work

  2. Bleed the compressed air manually by rotating the FESTO switch valve. .Bleed the compressed air Fig. 4200

    |

  1. Remove the complete set of bulkheads.

  2. In the respective section, unfold the cover sheet forward. Replace cylinder and flaps of suction channel (S02) .Fig. 4201

    | |

|1 Grid |2 Foldable protective plate

  1. Pull out the 2x circlips and push the axis out laterally. .Fig. 4202

    | |

  1. Remove the screw on the cover sheets on both sides. .Fig. 4203

    |

  1. Turn the connecting pin of the fork end and remove it. .Fig. 4204

    |

  1. Disconnect the two pneumatic hoses and if necessary, make a mark. .Fig. 4205

    | |

|1 Lock nut |2 Groove of piston rod |3 Pneumatic connector |

  1. With the first open-end wrench, hold the groove of the piston rod of the cylinder, and with the second open-end wrench, loosen the lock nut.

  2. Disconnect the piston rod of the cylinder by turning the fork head. .Fig. 4206

    |

  1. Remove the circlip from the mounting axis of the cylinder andpull the axis out laterally. .Fig. 4207

    | |

|1 Circlip |2 Mounting axis

  1. Take out the cylinder.

  2. To install a new cylinder: perform the removal steps of the cylinder in the reverse order.

  3. If the flap is defective or deformed, screw the screws out of the flap bolt and remove the flap. .Fig. 4208

    | |

|1 Flap |2 Screws, flap bolt |3 Screws, flap stop |

  1. Mount a new flap: perform the removal steps in the reverse order.

    • The flap must have contact with the flap stop when it is closed.

    • Make the flap system leak-tight to achieve the optimal suction power.

  2. If there is an air gap between the flap and flap stop, the lock nut is loosened and the piston rod is turned in the desired direction.

  3. In the respective section, dismount the protective covers overlapping the cover sheets. Replace cylinder and flaps of suction channel (S01) .Remove protective cover Fig. 4209

    | |

|1 Protective cover |2 Grid |3 Cover sheet, cylinder |

  1. Remove the cover sheet by loosening screws (4x).

  2. Lift the grid upwards and remove it. .Fig. 4210

    |

  1. Turn the connecting pin of the rod fork end and remove it. .Fig. 4211

    | |

|1 Connecting pin |2 Rod fork end

  1. Disconnect the two pneumatic hoses.

  2. Loosen the screws (3x) to dismantle the cylinder mounting plate. .Example (with pneumatic hoses and connecting pin) Fig. 4212

    | |

|1 Groove, piston rod |2 Lock nut

  1. With the first open-end wrench holding the groove of the piston rod of the cylinder, and with the second open-end wrench, loosen the lock nut.

  2. Dismount the cylinder from the mounting plate.

  3. To install a new cylinder: perform the removal steps of the cylinder in the reverse order.

  4. If the flap is defective or deformed, screw the screws out of the flap bolt and remove the flap. .Fig. 4213

    | |

|1 Screws, flap bolt |2 Flap |3 Screws, flap stop |

  1. Mount a new flap: perform the removal steps in the reverse order.

    • The flap must have contact with the flap stop when it is closed.

    • Make the flap system leak-tight to achieve the optimal suction power.

  2. If there is an air gap between the flap and flap stop, the lock nut is loosened and the piston rod is turned in the desired direction.

  3. Move both pallets into the pallet changer. Suction channel function

  4. Move the X axis to the maximum X position.

  5. In the switch cabinet, turn the current limit key switch to the right position. .Fig. 4214

    |

  1. Acknowledge the service key is active at the bottom-left of HMI. .Fig. 4215

    |

  1. Press the wrench icon and select PField Parameter in HMI. .Fig. 4216

    |

  1. Select Consecutive and enter 1980 under PField index. .Fig. 4217

    |

  1. Control the flaps one after the other.

    • Value 1 means: First flap is open (if the value is increased by one, the next flap opens, etc.).

    • Value 99 means: All flaps are open.

  2. Check all the flaps in the work area for correct function (check tight closing) and adjust flaps if necessary.

    • If a flap does not work: check pneumatic and/or electrical components (valve terminal or hose system, etc.).

  3. In the switch cabinet, turn the current limit key switch to the left.
