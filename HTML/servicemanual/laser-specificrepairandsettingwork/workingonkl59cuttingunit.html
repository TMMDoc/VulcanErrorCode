Working on KL59 cutting unit

Replacing the motor for lens adjustment, KL59

  • The power supply to the cutting unit is disconnected.

  • Compressed air supply is switched off.

  • The cutting unit and the area around it must be clean. Means, Tools, Materials

  • Loctite 242/243.

  • Torque screwdriver 1 Nm.

  • 12 Nm torque wrench, AF 12.

  • The following work can be performed with the cutting unit either removed or installed.

    1. If the cutting unit is installed: unplug CAN bus and voltage supply. Remove the motor

    2. Remove the compressed air supply from the cylinder. | a|

Loose spring on the cylinder Spring may fall down * Remove the spring.

  1. Remove the compressed air cylinder. .Compressed air cylinder with spring Fig. 3‑59

| a|

|1 Recirculating ball nut |2 Slide top position

  1. Remove the motor.

  2. Before installing the new motor: clean the flange surfaces/ support area on cutting unit and motor. Mount the motor

  3. Align connection cable of the new motor at the front.

  4. Place a drop of Loctite 242/243 on the recirculating ball nut and insert the motor

  5. Screw the ball nut to a torque of 12 Nm.

  6. Fit the motor screws.

  7. By moving the slide, align the motor in its uppermost position.

  8. Tighten the motor screws to a torque of 1 Nm.

  9. Manually move the slide to check its function.

  10. If slide is difficult to move, detach the motor and realign it.

  11. Screw on the electronics lid.

  12. Plug in the motor cable.

Currently we check the lens replacement manually by the program “Focal Distance”.

  1. Go to HMI, open the program “Focal Distance”.

  2. Acknowledge the current focus diameter (e.g., 150 µm). .Fig. 3‑71
